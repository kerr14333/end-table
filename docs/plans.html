Rough material: about 4–5 board feet of 3/4" cherry, plus a little waste for the taper and glue-up. The top is the only glue-up.

Joinery Details

Half-lap (leg crossing)

  • The two legs of each X cross at ≈ 57° (acute angle).
  • Crossing center is ≈ 13" up from the floor — about 14¾" from the foot measured along the board.
  • Lay the legs crossed, scribe the overlap, set a dado/router to exactly 3/8" (half of 3/4"), and remove the overlap from opposite faces so they interlock flush.
  • Glue the lap; clamp flat and check the frame sits without rocking.

Top edges (router)

  • Corners: 1⅛" radius — bandsaw/jigsaw close, then template or sand to the line.
  • Underside chamfer: 45° bearing-guided chamfer bit, depth set to 1/2", run around the whole perimeter (follows the rounded corners automatically). Leaves a 1/4" edge so the overhang reads thin.
  • Top edge: 1/8" roundover bit to soften the upper arris.
  • Cherry tears out easily — take light passes and climb-cut the corners.

Leg ends

The legs lean ≈ 28° from vertical. Cut the foot and top ends flat/horizontal (about a 28°-off-square cut on the miter saw) so the feet sit flat on the floor and the top ends bear squarely against the underside of the top.

Assembly Sequence

  1. Top: glue up the panel, flatten, trim to 20 × 8¼. Round the 4 corners, rout the underside 45° chamfer, then the 1/8" top roundover. Sand to 220.
  2. Leg blanks: rip four ~3"-wide blanks, mark the taper (2½" at the top to 1¼" at the foot), and cut/joint the tapers.
  3. Half-laps: lay out and cut the lap on each leg; dry-assemble the two X-frames and confirm the tips and feet are coplanar (no rock), then glue.
  4. Stretcher: join the two X-frames with the 3/4" stretcher at the crossing (glue + screws or dowels into the inner faces). This locks the base against racking.
  5. Attach the top (see note below).
  6. Finish: natural Danish oil or wiped satin poly. Cherry will darken to a warm reddish-brown that complements the chair.

Attaching the top — allow for wood movement. The solid 8¼" top will expand/contract across its width (~1/16" seasonally). Recommended: fasten a small cleat (~7 × 1½ × 3/4) under each end across the width — screw it rigidly to the leg tips, then attach it to the top with screws in elongated slots running across the grain (center hole snug, outer holes slotted) so the top can float. This also ties the two frames together at the top for extra rigidity. Avoid screwing the legs straight up into the top — that can crack the panel over time.

Design Notes

  • No breadboard ends. The top is a single solid panel; at 8¼" wide it is not very cup-prone, and breadboard ends would add an advanced joint and change the clean look. Can be added if desired.
  • Stability: feet are splayed past the top tips for a 14" lengthwise stance; the stretcher handles the narrower across-width direction.
  • Tools used: tablesaw, router, miter saw, and hand tools only.
